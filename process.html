After an ER visit and intensive googling,
I found myself with 6 period apps, some ovarian cysts
& a long list of questions.

My goal with this project was to create a
female health app that was straightforward,
educational & visually appealing.

Here's how it happened.

the research.

In my research, I found that most apps fell into 1 of 2
categories: great design, poor functionality OR great
functionality, poor design. Here is a brief overview of the
three leading female health apps:

From this I learned: most period apps have a daily view,
calendar view & profile, generally listed in that order.
They have some sort of forum/article section for
troubleshooting. It’s easy for data displays & symptom
tracking to become complicated.

the flow.

First, users will arrive on a sign-in/sign-up page and
are then directed to a daily view––where they can log
symptoms. or learn about relevant info. The three other
tabs are data, information & profile. Here are the XD
wireframes:

see annotated wireframes

the design.

The primary accent colors will be light pink and navy
blue, to symbolize femeninity & empathy alongside
knowledge & trust. The San Francisco font is clean
& cohesive with the rest of the iOS system

the mockup.

testing.

Four users from the target audience (young women)
were given three tasks that I thought were more
obscure within the prototype. They were (1) finding
cycle data, (2) use of info page & (3) the share
feature. From this I learned that finding the cycle data
is not intuitive & that the info page layout needs
revision
